The Choice, in depth

What home birth really offers

Home birth isn't about doing without — it's about a different kind of care, grounded in evidence and centered on you. Here's what that looks like with an experienced midwife.

Time and continuity

A midwife typically supports only a few families a month — so your visits are unhurried and the person at your birth is the person who knows your story.

Evidence‑based options

Movement, hydrotherapy, and different positions in labor are supported here — safe options many families aren't offered elsewhere.

A calm, familiar space

Labor in your own home, on your own terms, surrounded by the people you choose.

Full newborn care

Your midwife returns after birth to check on you and baby and performs the routine newborn screenings — the same ones done in a hospital.

Trained for the unexpected

Experienced midwives are trained in emergency response and recognize when a situation needs a higher level of care.

Support well beyond the birth

Postpartum visits over 6–8 weeks cover healing, newborn care, and feeding — you're never sent off alone.
