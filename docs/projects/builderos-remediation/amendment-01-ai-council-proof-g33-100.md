### Proof-Closing Blueprint Note: AI Council Charter (G33-100)

This note addresses the first foundational deliverable identified in `docs/projects/AMENDMENT_01_AI_COUNCIL.md` under "Initial Deliverables (Phase 1)": the "AI Council Charter". Establishing this charter is the smallest, safest, and most critical next step to formalize the AI Council's existence and operational framework within BuilderOS.

**1. Exact Missing Implementation or Proof Gap:**
The formal definition and establishment of the AI Council's operational framework, purpose, scope, membership, and operating procedures, as documented in the "AI Council Charter". This charter is a prerequisite for the council to function effectively and for subsequent deliverables (e.g., initial AI principles, review process definition, sub-committee formations) to have a legitimate governing body.

**2. Smallest Safe Build Slice to Close It:**
Draft, review, and finalize the `AI Council Charter` document. This slice focuses purely on governance documentation and does not involve any code changes to LifeOS or BuilderOS features. It defines the "who, what, when, where, why, and how" of the AI Council.

**3. Exact Safe-Scope Files to Touch First:**
*   `docs/governance/ai-council-charter.md` (new file, containing the charter draft)
*   `docs/projects/AMENDMENT_01_AI_COUNCIL.md` (update to reference the new charter document and mark this deliverable as in progress/completed)

**4. Verifier/Runtime Checks:**
*   **Leadership Review:** Formal approval of the charter by BuilderOS leadership to ensure strategic alignment and mandate.
*   **Legal & Compliance Review:** Verification by legal and compliance teams for adherence to internal policies and external regulatory requirements.
*   **Stakeholder Consultation:** Engagement with potential AI Council members and affected BuilderOS teams to gather feedback and ensure practical operability.
*   **Internal Publication:** The finalized charter is published to a designated, accessible location within BuilderOS documentation.

**5. Stop Conditions if Runtime Truth Disagrees:**
*   **Fundamental Rejection:** If BuilderOS leadership rejects the core mandate, structure, or necessity of the AI Council as proposed in the charter.
*   **Legal Non-Compliance:** If legal or compliance reviews identify critical, unresolvable non-starters that fundamentally prevent the council from operating as intended.
*   **Operational Impasse:** If key stakeholders (e.g., potential council members) raise insurmountable objections to the proposed operating model, preventing effective formation or function.
*   **Timeline Breach:** If the charter cannot be finalized and approved within a defined, reasonable timeframe (e.g., 2-4 weeks), indicating a deeper disagreement on the council's viability or priority.
